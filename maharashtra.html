Agriculture in Maharashtra

Maharashtra is one of the leading agricultural states in India, known for its diverse range of crops, including sugarcane, cotton, and various fruits. The state's agriculture is primarily rain-fed, with significant reliance on the southwest monsoon.

Challenges

Farmers in Maharashtra face challenges such as water scarcity, soil degradation, and vulnerability to climate change. Erratic rainfall patterns and frequent droughts further exacerbate these issues.

Solutions

To combat these challenges, initiatives like drip irrigation and rainwater harvesting are being promoted. Farmers are also encouraged to adopt sustainable practices and diversify their crops for better resilience.

Major Crops

  • Sugarcane
  • Cotton
  • Rice
  • Wheat
  • Millets

Minor Crops

  • Pulses
  • Fruits (Mango, Banana)
  • Vegetables
  • Groundnut
  • Onions
