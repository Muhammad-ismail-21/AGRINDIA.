Agriculture in Karnataka

Karnataka is a major agricultural state in India, known for its diverse range of crops and innovative farming practices. The state's agricultural economy is supported by various climatic zones, making it suitable for both major and minor crops.

Karnataka's diverse climate ranges from tropical in the coastal regions to semi-arid in the interior. The state has a variety of soil types, including black soil, which is ideal for cotton cultivation, and red soil, conducive for pulses and cereals.

Agriculture in Karnataka plays a pivotal role in the state's economy and livelihood. With diverse agro-climatic conditions, Karnataka is one of India’s leading agricultural states, contributing significantly to the production of various crops. The state's agriculture is characterized by a mix of both traditional and modern practices, allowing for the cultivation of a wide range of crops including cereals, pulses, oilseeds, and cash crops like coffee, sugarcane, and horticultural products.
